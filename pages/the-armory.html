The Tools of Dominion

The Harness of the Sun

⚜

The Bascinet

A pointed steel helm designed to deflect arrows and glancing blows. It features a "Klappvisor" for breathing, though many knights throw it up for better vision at the cost of safety.

The Cuirass

Two plates of hardened steel (breast and back) protecting the vital organs.

The Gauntlets

Articulated steel gloves lined with leather, allowing the knight to grip his weapon while protecting the fingers from being severed.

The Sabatons

Steel footwear, often pointed, to protect the feet from infantry maces.

The Care of Arms

⚜

A knight's weapons are his life. A poorly maintained blade may snap at the crucial moment; rusted armor may seal a knight in his own harness. Every squire must master these arts:

Daily Care

  • Wipe blade with oiled cloth after each use
  • Check all straps and buckles for wear
  • Airdry damp leather components
  • Inspect for loose rivets or bent parts

Weekly Care

  • Full oiling of all metal components
  • Condition leather with rendered tallow
  • Polish blade to mirror finish
  • Test shield boss for firmness

Monthly Care

  • Disassemble and deep-clean harness
  • Replace worn straps and padding
  • Reseat all rivets with brass pins
  • Regrease articulated joints

Famous Blades of Legend

⚜

Throughout history, certain swords have achieved immortality alongside their wielders:

Excalibur

The Sword in the Stone, later pulled from the lake by Arthur himself. Its blade shone with the light of thirty suns. It was returned to the Lady of the Lake upon Arthur's final battle.

Durandal

Carried by Roland at the Battle of Roncevaux Pass. When all seemed lost, Roland tried to shatter it against a mountain rather than let it fall to enemy hands. The resulting notch became known as the "Brèche de Roland."

Joyeuse

The sword of Charlemagne, said to change colour thirty times in a day. The blade was believed to contain a splinter of the True Cross within its golden hilt.

Murglei

Forged by the dwarfs Dvalin and Dain, this blade never failed its wielder. It sang softly when drawn and never grew dull, though it could not cut stone or iron.

The Arsenal of the Just

⚜

The Longsword

A double-edged blade roughly 45 inches in length. It is used with one or two hands and is the "Sword of Justice."

The Poleaxe

The knight's weapon of choice on foot. It features an axe-head for hacking, a hammer-head for crushing plate, and a top-spike for piercing visors.

The Heater Shield

A triangular shield made of layered wood and leather, used to deflect lances and display heraldry.

The Rondel Dagger

A stiff, needle-like blade used to finish a fallen opponent by sliding between the gaps in their armor.

The Weight of Honour

⚜

A full harness of plate armour could weigh between 45 and 60 pounds—yet a well-fitted knight could run, vault, and fight with remarkable agility. Consider:

60 lbs Full Harness
8 ft Lance Length
45 in Sword Blade

Yet the true weight a knight carries is not iron, but obligation. Every piece of steel is a promise made to those who cannot defend themselves.

The Art of Combat

⚜

A knight must master multiple disciplines, each suited to different circumstances of battle:

On Horseback

The lance rules the charge. Speed and aim determine victory. Once the lance is shattered, the knight draws his sword, using the momentum of the horse to deliver devastating blows.

On Foot

The poleaxe becomes paramount. Here the fight is brutal and close. The hammer crushes; the axe cleaves; the spike pierces visor and seam.

The Judicial Duel

God decides the outcome. Here honour is wagered on steel alone. The duel continues until one yields, falls, or dies—though yielding is rarer than death.

The Forge Master's Art

⚜

A master blacksmith holds the key to a knight's survival. The finest blades are forged with techniques passed down through generations:

Damascus Steel

Folded layers of iron and steel create a blade of unparalleled strength, with water-like patterns flowing across its surface.

Tempering

Heat and oil transform brittle steel into resilient metal that bends without breaking.

Fullering

The groove down the center of a blade is not merely decorative—it reduces weight while maintaining structural strength.

Pattern Welding

Multiple rods of iron are twisted and folded, creating blades of remarkable durability and beauty.
