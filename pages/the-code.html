The Code

The Sacred Vows of the Virtuous

The Foundation of the Soul

⚜

To wear the spurs is to accept a burden heavier than any plate armor. A knight's life is governed by the Lex Chivalria, a triple-bond of duty to God, Suzerain, and the Vulnerable.

The Twelve Pillars of the Code

⚜

Faith

To believe all the Church teaches and observe its directions.

The knight begins each day with prayer and ends it with thanksgiving. His faith is his compass in the darkest hour of battle.

Valour

To never retreat before the enemy; to seek glory through deeds, not words.

Courage is not the absence of fear, but the mastery of it. The knight must be the last to stand when all others flee.

Compassion

To be the shield of the widow and the father of the orphan.

Strength without mercy is mere brutality. The knight's sword serves those who cannot wield it themselves.

Justice

To side always with the Right against Injustice and Evil.

Not merely the law, but the spirit of it. The knight must discern truth in the lies and fairness in deceit.

Prudence

To seek wisdom before the sword and temper wrath with reason.

A thousand battles won through counsel are worth more than one won through bloodshed.

Courtesy

To treat even an enemy with the respect due to their station.

Even the vilest serf deserves dignity. A knight's manners are his mirror—reflecting either honour or shame.

Largesse

To be generous to the poor and to despise the hoarding of gold.

Gold is merely the measure of a transaction, not the measure of a man. The knight who hoards is already dead inside.

Loyalty

To remain true to one's liege lord until the final breath.

The bond between knight and sovereign transcends death itself. A broken oath can never be remade.

Honour

To keep one's word as a sacred bond; a knight's oath is his life.

A knight without honour is merely a murderer with better equipment. His word is his most prized possession.

Hardiness

To endure cold, hunger, and fatigue without complaint.

The body is the vessel of the spirit. Train it to endure, and the mind shall never break.

Humility

To shun the sin of pride and give credit for victory to the Almighty.

No knight stands alone. The victory belongs to God, to his brothers, and to those who believed when no one else did.

Nobility

To behave in a manner that elevates the name of one's lineage.

Your ancestors built their names brick by brick. It takes but a moment of shame to tear down generations of honour.

The Knight's Oath

⚜

Before dubbing, every aspirant must speak these words upon their knees before a sworn knight of the Order:

"I am a man who has no lord. I come to you to ask for the order of knighthood, that I may be worthy of the spurs. I swear upon my honour, upon my soul, and upon my ancestors, that I shall defend the faith, protect the innocent, and uphold the Code until my last breath. Should I ever break this oath, may my name be struck from the Book of the Round, and may I be forgotten by all who came before and all who shall follow."

Knights of Legend

⚜

These warriors exemplify the virtues of the Code, their names etched in the annals of history:

Sir William Marshal

Served four kings of England and remained loyal to each unto death. His prowess at tournaments earned him the title "The Greatest Knight in the World." Even in his final years, he rode to battle against the King's enemies, for a knight's duty never ends.

Sir Richard the Lionheart

A warrior-king who personified Valour. He fought in over a dozen major battles and never once asked for quarter. His very name—Lionheart—speaks to the fire that burned within him.

Sir William Wallace

Though never knighted by the crown, he embodied Compassion and Justice. He fought not for glory, but for the freedom of his people. "Freedom" was his battle cry, and it echoes still.

The Seven Deadly Sins

⚜

These are the poisons that corrupt a knight's soul. Every virtuous knight must guard against these internal enemies:

Pride

The deadliest sin. To believe oneself better than others is to forget that all honour comes from God.

Wrath

Rage unchecked becomes cruelty. The knight must temper fury with reason, striking only for justice.

Sloth

To neglect one's duties is to betray one's oath. Training must be daily; service must be constant.

Avarice

The knight who hoards wealth loses sight of his purpose. Take what you need; give the rest away.

Gluttony

The body must be honed, not indulged. Excess weakens the vessel that carries the spirit.

Lust

Passion unchecked leads to broken oaths and ruined honour. Temperance preserves dignity.

Envy

To covet another's glory is to diminish your own worth. Each knight's path is unique.

Wisdom of the Ages

⚜

"A knight is sworn to be: defender of the faith, protector of the realm, and servant to justice. These are not choices—they are obligations written in blood and stone."

— The Book of Chivalry, 1284

"The sword is the last argument of kings. But the true weapon of the knight is honour—and honour cannot be taken, only earned."

— Sir Geoffrey de Charny, 1350

"Better to die with honour than to live with shame. For death is brief, but the memory of dishonour endures forever."

— The Code of the Round Table

"A knight without courtesy is merely a bully with expensive equipment. The Code without manners is a sword without a scabbard—dangerous to all, including the wielder."

— Master-at-Arms Aldric of York
