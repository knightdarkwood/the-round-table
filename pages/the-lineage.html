⚜

Heraldry has its own grammar, spoken by heralds across the realm. To read a coat of arms is to read a family's story:

Party per pale: Divided vertically into two equal parts

In base: The lower portion of the shield

The Rules of Marshalling

⚜

When houses combine through marriage or alliance, their arms must be marshalled—combined according to strict rules:

Impaling

Two shields side by side, divided vertically. Used for a married couple—the husband's arms on the dexter (right), wife's on the sinister (left).

Quartering

The shield divided into four, with different arms in each quarter. Used for complex lineages of multiple inheritances.

Dimidiation

Half of one coat joined to half of another. Less common, often used in uncertain claims of lineage.
