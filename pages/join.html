The Oath of Induction

⚜

Before you stand ready to swear fealty, hear the words you shall speak upon your knees before the assembled Knights:

"I, [Your Name], do hereby pledge my sword to the Round Table. I swear to uphold the Code of Chivalry, to defend the weak, to serve my brothers in honour, and to never disgrace the name I now bear. Until my final breath, I am bound."

Requirements of knighthood

⚜

Pure Intent

You must come not for glory or gold, but to serve the greater good and uphold the ancient virtues.

Letters of Recommendation

Two current members of the Order must vouch for your character and your dedication to the Code.

Proven Skill

You must demonstrate competence in combat or an equivalent discipline that serves the Order's mission.

The Three Paths of Service

⚜

Not all knights wear the same harness. Choose your calling:

The Shield

Those who take up the sword in defense of the realm. You shall train at the lists, maintain your arms, and answer when the trumpet sounds.

The Parchment

Those who wield the pen rather than the blade. Scribes, chroniclers, and advisors who preserve the knowledge of the ages.

The Purse

Those who fund our ventures. Patrons and benefactors whose generosity keeps the hearths burning and the horses fed.

Swear Your Oath

⚜

Should you meet the requirements and wish to proceed, petition the High Council. Your name shall be entered into the Ledger of Aspirants.

Common Questions

⚜

Do I need noble blood to join?

No. The Round Table was founded on the principle that honour is earned, not inherited. Many of our greatest brothers rose from humble origins.

How long does the induction process take?

The petition is reviewed within thirty days. The full induction—including the Trial and Vigil—typically spans three months.

Is the Order religious?

While we respect all faiths, our Code is rooted in Christian tradition. Brothers are expected to maintain their own spiritual practice.

What is expected of a Brother?

Monthly dues, attendance at the Gathering, and a commitment to live by the Code in all your dealings—personal, professional, and public.

The Degrees of Knighthood

⚜

There are three degrees within the Order, each representing a deeper commitment:

Squire Initiate

First Year

You learn the ways of the Order. Mentorship by a senior brother. Limited duties, maximum observation.

Knight Companion

Full Member

The heart of the Order. Voting rights, chapter membership, full duties and privileges.

Knight Commander

By Appointment

Leadership positions, command of chapters, voice in the High Council.
