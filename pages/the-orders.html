The Orders

✦

The Iron Brotherhoods

The Poor Fellow-Soldiers of Christ

The Templars

⚜

Founded in 1119 to protect pilgrims, they became the most powerful force in Christendom. They lived as monks—taking vows of poverty, chastity, and obedience—but fought as the elite heavy cavalry of the Crusades. Their signature white mantle with a red cross struck fear into the hearts of their enemies.

The Order of the Hospital of St. John

The Hospitallers

⚜

Originally founded to care for the sick in Jerusalem, they evolved into a formidable naval and military power. They wore black surcoats with white crosses. Even in the heat of war, they maintained their hospitals, proving that a knight's hand can both heal and slay.

The Order of the Garter

England's Most Prestigious Order

⚜

The most prestigious order of the English Crown, founded by Edward III in 1348. Inspired by the legends of King Arthur, it is limited to the Sovereign and twenty-four "Companions."

"Honi soit qui mal y pense"

Shame on him who thinks evil of it

This defines the elite standard of aristocratic chivalry.

A Knight's Daily Devotions

⚜

Regardless of their order, all knights follow a rhythm of prayer and duty:

Matins First prayers at dawn, giving thanks for another day
Prime Morning prayers before the day's duties begin
Tierce Mid-morning prayers, asking for strength
Sext Midday prayers, the sun at its peak
Nones Afternoon prayers, reflecting on the day's deeds
Vespers Evening prayers, giving thanks for protection
Compline Final prayers before sleep, seeking forgiveness

The Four Humilities

⚜

Beyond the Code, knights of certain orders embrace the Four Humilities—teachings that separate the warrior from the saint:

To Fear God

Not fear as cowardice, but awe—a recognition that all power comes from on high.

To Honour the Priest

To place God's servants above all earthly power, for they speak for the divine.

To Shun Wickedness

To not merely avoid evil, but to actively oppose it in all its forms.

To Keep One's Oath

The most sacred of obligations—the word given must be the word kept.

Great Battles of the Orders

⚜

These engagements defined the military legacy of the great orders:

1177

The Battle of Montgisard

Outnumbered five to one, the Templars under Baldwin IV routed Saladin's forces. The young king, afflicted by leprosy, led the charge himself.

1241

The Battle of Mohi

The Hospitallers held the bridge against the Mongol horde, giving time for refugees to flee. Most of the knights died in place.

1346

The Battle of Crécy

Knights of the Garter fought alongside the English king, their banners flying as the flower of French knighthood was cut down.

The Secret Signs

⚜

Brothers of the same order recognise each other through subtle signs—a touch to the collar, a specific grip, a phrase spoken at dawn:

The Templar Grip

Thumb pressed to the second knuckle of the index finger, as if holding a sword in prayer.

The Hospitaller Sign

Three fingers extended, representing the three vows: poverty, chastity, and obedience.

The Garter Phrase

"Honi soit"—spoken in response to any slight against one's honour.
