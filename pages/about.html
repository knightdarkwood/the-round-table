The History

✦

A Thousand Years of Honour

The Founding

⚜

In the year of our Lord 1027, a council was held in the shadowed halls of Camelot. King Arthur had seen the chaos that befell a realm without unity—no shield to defend the weak, no law to restrain the strong. And so, he commanded the crafting of a great round table, that no man might sit above another.

From this table came the first Knights of the Order: warriors bound not by gold or fear, but by an oath sworn unto each other. The Code was etched not in stone, but in the hearts of those who dared to live by it.

The Great Schism

⚜

The Order weathered many storms. In the winter of 1148, when treachery split the realm asunder, the Knights of the Round Table stood divided. Half marched with the true King; half were deceived by the serpent's whisper.

The war that followed lasted seven years. When it ended, the survivors swore a new oath: never again would the Order fracture. The Code was strengthened, and a new tradition began—the annual Gathering at the Table, where all brothers must sit as equals, regardless of standing.

The Modern Order

⚜

The ways of war have changed. The lance has given way to the engine, the castle wall to the firewall. But the virtues endure. Mercy. Valor. Truth. These are not relics of a forgotten age—they are the bedrock upon which honour is built.

Today, the Round Table endures as a fellowship of those who refuse to let the darkness win. We are bankers and builders, scholars and soldiers, united not by blood but by conviction. Our enemies wear not armour, but apathy. Our battles are fought not on fields of gore, but in the daily choice to do what is right.

Our Holdings

⚜

The Order maintains several strongholds across the realm:

The Citadel of Northmoor

Our ancestral seat in the Northern Marches. Home to the Great Hall and the Round Table itself.

The Priory of St. Aldric

A monastery-fortress in the Eastern valleys, training ground for our youngest squires.

The Harbourwatch of Saltmere

Our naval outpost, guarding the western shipping lanes from corsairs and rogues.

The Blackwood Lodge

A hidden retreat in the ancient forest, used for councils that must remain secret.

The Great Chronicles

⚜

Throughout the ages, certain events have shaped the Order into what it is today:

1027

The Founding of the Round Table by King Arthur at Camelot. Nine knights sworn to the first oath.

1088

The First Crusade. The Order sends brothers to the Holy Land, forging bonds with the Templars.

1148

The Great Schism. Seven years of civil war within the Order. The survivors forge the Second Oath.

1215

The signing of the Magna Carta. Knights of the Order stand as witnesses to history.

1348

The Black Death. The Order loses a third of its brothers, but survives to carry on.

1485

The Wars of the Roses end. The Order remains neutral, preserving unity for the realm.

The Round Table Today

⚜

In these modern times, the Order continues its mission in new forms:

Defence of the Vulnerable

We support legal aid for those who cannot afford it. The law must serve all, not only those with coin.

Preservation of Honour

We mentor young men and women in the ways of integrity, responsibility, and service.

Brothers in Arms

We support veterans returning from service, ensuring that those who served are never forgotten.

"The sword may have changed—its edge now lies in the boardroom as much as the battlefield—but the duty remains: to stand between darkness and light, and to never flinch."

The Order's Rivals

⚜

Throughout history, the Round Table has faced those who would see honour extinguished:

The Shadow Covenant

A secret society of mercenaries who serve gold, not honour. They wear no colours, bear no arms openly. They strike from darkness.

The Iron Wolves

A northern order of berserkers who despise all notions of chivalry. They burn libraries and murder priests.

The Silver Tongues

Merchants and politicians who corrupt honour for profit. Their weapons are contracts and lies, not swords.
